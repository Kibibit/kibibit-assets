Current snapshot based on 28 usable responses from 37 raw records, including screenouts and incompletes.

Executive Summary

The clearest signal from this survey is that teams have already adopted AI, but most have not operationalized it. Respondents report fragmented AI usage across chats, local sessions, and tools; repeated prompts and duplicated context; weak measurement of impact; and rising pressure from leadership to justify spend and outcomes.

The strongest demand is not for more AI access, but for a system that enables visibility, comparison, reuse, auditability, and coordination. That aligns directly with Shepherd’s product thesis.

Implications for Shepherd

  1. Cross-tool visibility is a foundational need. Teams are already using multiple AI tools, but cannot see work in one place.
  2. Measurement is a gating capability. Leadership is asking for ROI before most teams can produce credible evidence.
  3. Workflow reuse is a stronger wedge than reporting alone. The highest-rated capability is turning successful sessions into reusable playbooks.
  4. Comparison and optimization are central. Respondents want to know which prompts, agents, models, and workflows actually perform best.
  5. Human-in-the-loop orchestration resonates. Strong interest in human-controlled coordination suggests a practical adoption path versus fully autonomous systems.

Investor Narrative

Teams have adopted AI, but the work is not yet operationalized.

They use multiple tools, repeat context, manage work individually, and struggle to identify which prompts, agents, or workflows actually work. At the same time, leadership is increasingly asking for proof of cost, usage, and business impact.

The strongest demand is not for more model access. It is for a system of record and control layer for AI work — one that supports reuse, comparison, measurement, and coordination.

That is Shepherd’s wedge: help teams move from fragmented AI activity to measurable, reusable, team-level AI operations.

Suggested One-Line Positioning

Shepherd helps teams operationalize AI work across tools by making workflows measurable, reusable, auditable, and comparable.
